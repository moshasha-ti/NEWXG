Know about us

We are a legal consulting company based in Johannesburg, South Africa. We provide immigration-related services and professional legal advice.


XG Advisory is headed by Adv. Xolani Gila, an admitted Advocate of the High Court of South Africa. He has an LLB degree, additional qualifications in legislative drafting, compliance management and international business transactions. Adv. Gila has gained valuable experience in his capacity as a law practitioner in both the public and private sectors. His areas of specialty include family and immigration law and mediation.


Our founding philosophy at XG Advisory is to uphold the Constitution of the Republic of South Africa and play our part in protecting the rights enshrined therein, including the right to accessible legal representation. We strongly believe in and encourage mediation and negotiation in our legal approach. We pride ourselves in protecting our clients' interest by providing efficient and cost effective legal services.

frequently asked questions

Who should pay maintenance??

It is a legal obligation of both the biological parents, grandparents or legal guardian of the child to pay maintenance.

Is maintenance a responsibility of biological parents only?

No, any person who is responsible to raise the child i.e. legal guardian, adoptive parents and grandparents of the child, if the biological parents do not have means to pay maintenance.

How often should maintenance be paid?

The maintenance money should be paid every month on the dates agreed upon by both parties and granted by the court.

How much money should be paid for maintenance?

The amount payable for maintenance will be determined by the needs of the child and the financial means of the parents/parties.

Can I increase/decrease the maintenance amount after the order has been granted?

Yes, you can request decrease/increase of the amounts, especially if the financial circumstances have changed.
